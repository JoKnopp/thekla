But any strategy must encompass more than a decision on how many troops or diplomats to deploy it must also provide guidance for how those decisions will be supported at home. When our country goes to war , we must also have a plan for how to pay for it. From the War of 1812 to the Civil War , World Wars I and II , Korea , and Vietnam , the government has consistently increased taxes or reduced domestic spending in varying forms to pay for most of the cost of American military engagement .
