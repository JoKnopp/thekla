When you go into the Army with children , they make you execute a form that says , " When I deploy my kids will be cared for by so-and-so. " " So-and-so " has to sign the form too , so it 's not like " so-and-so " is n't aware of the arrangement. Usually , in a husband-wife scenario , one soldier is deployed while the other stays home. This is why spouses with children are n't in the same units. This is also how they work it with single parents when both soldiers are on active duty -- one is assigned to a deployable unit , the other is not .
